Vegan Lomo Saltado

frommybowl.com2-3 Servings10min10min20min

A classic Peruvian dish, this Vegan Lomo Saltado uses Portobello Mushrooms to replicate this hearty, easy, and delicious main.

  • thick cut French Fries (or 1 cup Jasmine rice)
  • 2 cup Portobello Mushrooms caps, sliced into ½” pieces
  • 1 Red Onion, sliced
  • 2 Roma Tomatoes, sliced into ½” sticks
  • 1 clove of Garlic, minced
  • 3 tbsp Dry Red Wine (or 3tbsp red wine vinegar)
  • 3 tbsp Reduced-Sodium soy sauce
  • 1⁄2 tsp Cumin
  • 1⁄2 tsp Paprika
  • 1⁄2 tsp Oregano
  • 1 tsp Ají Panca Paste (or chipotles in adobo or harissa)
  • Salt
  • Ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp Fresh Parsley, finely chopped
  1. Wash and chop the portobello mushroom caps (1/2” slices), red onion (slices), and roma tomatoes(1/2” slices). Try to cut them evenly, so they all cook through at the same time. Mince the garlic and finely chop the parsley.
  2. Prep your ingredients. Measure out the dry spices together.
  3. Heat your Wok (or large sauté pan) over high heat, then add in 2 tbsp of high heat oil. Once the oil has heated, add the garlic to the wok and sauté for 10 or so seconds, then add in the sliced Mushrooms, dry spices, and a good pinch salt & pepper. Mix well, then pour the soy sauce & Red Wine into the wok as well. Sauté for 2 minutes, until the Mushrooms barely start to cook down and simmer.
  4. Add the sliced Red Onions and stir well; cook for 5 minutes, then add the chipotles in adobo to the wok and mix until well-combined. Finally, carefully add the sliced Tomatoes to the wok and gently sauté for 1 minute minute, just until the Tomatoes become well-heated and absorb some of the sauce. Turn the heat off (so the Tomatoes do not overcook) and transfer to serving dishes.
  5. Top with fresh Parsley and serve alongside French Fries and White Rice. Leftovers will keep in the fridge for up to 5 days.
